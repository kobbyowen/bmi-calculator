What is BMI(Body Mass Index)?

1. Introduction to BMI

BMI, or Body Mass Index, is a numerical value that helps determine whether a person has a healthy body weight in relation to their height. Iit is widely used by healthcare professionals and researchers to assess weight categories and possible health risks related to being underweight, normal weight, overweight, or obese.

2. Why is BMI Important?

BMI provides a simple and quick way to estimate whether a person is within a healthy weight range. Although it is not a perfect measurement, it helps identify potential health risks associated with body weight, such as:

Heart disease
Diabetes
High blood pressure
Joint problems
Metabolic disorders

Because BMI only considers weight and height, it does not measure body fat percentage, but it still serves as a useful screening tool.
